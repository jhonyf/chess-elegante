Example Commentary

1. e4 e5
2. Nf3 Nc6
3. Bb5 a6
4. Ba4 Nf6
5. O-O Be7

The Ruy Lopez unfolds with classical precision. White develops systematically—each move serving multiple purposes. The knight to f3 attacks the central e5 pawn while developing toward the kingside. The bishop to b5 maintains pressure on the c6 knight, which defends e5.

Black responds with equally principled moves. The a6-Ba4 maneuver demonstrates deep understanding: forcing White's bishop to declare its intentions while maintaining flexibility. This is chess at its most refined—a conversation between two minds across 64 squares.
